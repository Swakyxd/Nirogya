**Cholera**

*Symptoms*:
- Watery diarrhea (often called "rice-water stools")
- Vomiting
- Leg cramps
- Dehydration, which can lead to:
  - Rapid heart rate
  - Loss of skin elasticity
  - Dry mucous membranes
  - Low blood pressure
  - Shock
  - Coma
- Symptoms usually appear 2-3 days after infection, but can be as quick as a few hours or as long as 5 days.

*Causes*:
- Caused by the bacterium Vibrio cholerae.
- Spread through contaminated food or water.
- The bacteria can also live in brackish and coastal waters.
- Eating raw shellfish can be a source of infection.

*Prevention*:
- Access to safe water, basic sanitation, and hygiene (WASH) is crucial.
- Oral cholera vaccine (OCV) is available.
- Wash hands thoroughly with soap and water.
- Practice safe eating and drinking habits, especially when traveling.

**Hepatitis A**

*Symptoms*:
- Fever
- Malaise (general feeling of discomfort)
- Loss of appetite
- Diarrhea
- Nausea
- Abdominal discomfort
- Dark-colored urine
- Jaundice (yellowing of the skin and eyes)
- Not everyone infected will have all symptoms. Adults are more likely to show symptoms than children.
- Symptoms usually appear 2-7 weeks after exposure and last less than 2 months.

*Causes*:
- Caused by the hepatitis A virus (HAV).
- Spreads through close person-to-person contact.
- Spreads by eating contaminated food or drink (fecal-oral transmission).
- The virus can live on surfaces for a few months.

*Prevention*:
- Vaccination is the best way to prevent infection.
- Wash hands thoroughly with soap and water.
- Practice safe eating and drinking habits.

**Typhoid Fever**

*Symptoms*:
- Prolonged high fever
- Fatigue
- Headache
- Nausea
- Abdominal pain
- Constipation or diarrhea
- Some patients may have a rash.
- Severe cases can lead to serious complications or death.

*Causes*:
- Caused by the bacterium Salmonella Typhi.
- Spread through contaminated food or water.
- The bacteria live only in humans and are carried in the bloodstream and intestinal tract.

*Prevention*:
- Vaccination is available (oral and injectable).
- Practice safe eating and drinking habits.
- Wash hands thoroughly with soap and water.
- Ensure access to safe drinking water and adequate sanitation.
